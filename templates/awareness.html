Understanding Digital Threats

Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks use deceptive emails, messages, or websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or financial details.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Verify sender email addresses carefully
  • Don't click on suspicious links
  • Never share sensitive information via email
  • Use email filtering services
  • Check for HTTPS before entering credentials
Read More

Malware Protection

Malware can compromise your devices, steal personal data, and even hold your files hostage through ransomware attacks.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Keep operating systems and software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Be cautious of downloaded files
  • Back up important data regularly
  • Use ad-blockers to prevent malvertising
Read More

Password Security

Weak passwords are a common entry point for cyber attacks. Strong password practices are essential for digital security.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Create complex passwords (12+ characters)
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Use a password manager
  • Change passwords regularly
Read More

Social Engineering

Social engineering tricks people into giving away private info or access by exploiting trust and emotions.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Verify identities
  • Don't overshare
  • Question unusual requests
  • Stay informed
  • Trust your instincts
Read More

Public Wi-Fi Risks

Free Wi-Fi in public places can be unsafe, exposing your data to hackers and snoopers.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Avoid logging in to sensitive accounts
  • Use a VPN
  • Turn off sharing
  • Stick to HTTPS sites
  • Forget networks after use
Read More

Data Breaches

Data breaches expose personal info when companies or systems are hacked or misconfigured.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Enable 2FA
  • Monitor your accounts
  • Update software regularly
  • Limit data sharing

Digital Wellness & Balance

Screen Time Management

Excessive screen time can impact your physical and mental health. Finding balance is key to digital wellness.

Healthy Practices:

  • Set daily screen time limits
  • Use screen time monitoring apps
  • Take regular breaks (20-20-20 rule)
  • Create tech-free zones at home
  • Disconnect before bedtime
Read More

Mental Health Online

Social media and digital content can affect your mental health. Being aware of these impacts is the first step to managing them.

Healthy Practices:

  • Curate a positive feed
  • Be mindful of comparison traps
  • Practice digital detox regularly
  • Set boundaries for social media use
  • Seek support if experiencing cyberbullying
Read More

Information Literacy

In the age of misinformation, knowing how to evaluate online content and identify fake news is crucial.

Healthy Practices:

  • Check sources before sharing content
  • Verify information across multiple sources
  • Be wary of emotional headlines
  • Look for publication dates
  • Recognize sponsored content

Online Privacy Protection

Digital Footprint

Every action online leaves a trace. Understanding your digital footprint can help you manage your online presence.

Privacy Tips:

  • Regularly audit your online accounts
  • Search for yourself online
  • Adjust privacy settings on all platforms
  • Be cautious about personal information sharing
  • Consider using privacy-focused services
Read More

Secure Browsing

Your browsing habits can expose personal data. Taking steps to browse securely can enhance your privacy.

Privacy Tips:

  • Use a VPN for sensitive activities
  • Browse in private/incognito mode
  • Clear cookies and browsing history regularly
  • Use privacy-focused browsers
  • Install privacy protection extensions
Read More

Mobile Device Security

Smartphones contain a wealth of personal data. Securing these devices is essential for privacy.

Privacy Tips:

  • Use strong passcodes or biometrics
  • Review app permissions regularly
  • Turn off location services when not needed
  • Keep apps and OS updated
  • Use remote wipe capabilities

Frequently Asked Questions About Cybersecurity

Signs of a compromised account include unexpected account activity, emails about password changes you didn't request, friends receiving messages you didn't send, or inability to log in. Check if your email has been involved in data breaches using services like Have I Been Pwned.

Malware is a broad term for any malicious software designed to harm devices or networks. A virus is a specific type of malware that replicates by inserting its code into other programs. Other malware types include ransomware, spyware, adware, and trojans.

Protect kids online by maintaining open communication, using parental controls, teaching online privacy and safety, setting screen time rules, and staying informed about their apps and platforms.

Password managers generate and store strong, unique passwords, requiring only one master password. They prevent password reuse, thwart brute force attacks, and often include auto-fill, secure notes, and security alerts.

Two-factor authentication (2FA) enhances security by requiring two verification methods: a password and a second factor (e.g., a phone code). It prevents unauthorized access even if your password is stolen.
